# Cyber Security Guidelines

## Research & Learn

### What are common cyber security threats in a remote work environment?
Phishing, usually done through fake email IDs that try to trick you into clicking a link or 
giving away information.

### What are best practices for keeping your devices and accounts secure?
Using strong passwords, enabling two factor authentication, keeping software updated, and 
avoiding suspicious links or downloads.

### Why is it important to lock your computer when away from your desk?
If your computer is left unlocked, someone could access your accounts or company data without 
a password, which could lead to a data breach.

### How should you handle phishing attempts and suspicious links?
Do not click on links or open attachments from unknown or suspicious senders. Check the sender's 
email address carefully before trusting anything, and report it instead of interacting with it.

### What makes a strong password, and why should you use a password manager?
A strong password is long and unique, using a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. A password 
manager helps store and generate these passwords so you do not have to reuse the same one 
everywhere.

### Why is two-factor authentication (2FA) important, and when should you enable it?
2FA adds an extra layer of security beyond just a password. Even if someone gets your password, 
they still cannot log in without the second step. It should be enabled on all important accounts, 
especially work accounts.

### What steps will you take to ensure your passwords and accounts are secure?

### What would you do if you suspected a security breach or suspicious activity on your account?

report it to my supervisor so it can be looked into further.
